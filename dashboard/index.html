Oberon Guide & Concept

How Oberon Differs from Copernicus & Standard EO tools

Copernicus Browser and raw Sentinel APIs provide imagery search and raster downloads. However, transforming these into actionable decisions is complex. Oberon bridges this gap by acting as an end-to-end Evidence & Decision Engine.

1. Automatic Comparable Scene Triage

Instead of manually searching for cloudless scenes, Oberon ranks, cloud-masks, and selects locally optimal observations for your exact AOI boundary. If a single scene contains clouds, it falls back to composing median cloud-free pixels from up to 3 scenes.

2. Deterministic & AI-Enabled Change Ranking

Oberon calculates vegetation indices (NDVI, NBR) and performs signed thresholding to isolate real disturbance. If enabled, it runs the Clay v1.5 Geospatial Foundation Model alongside to compute deep feature distance maps that flag complex changes.

3. Spatial Variance Seasonality Masking

A common problem with change detection is seasonal leaf-shedding. Oberon computes the coefficient of variation (CV) of NDVI losses. A uniform drop across the AOI suggests autumn/winter senescence and is automatically abstained from, preventing false alerts.

4. The Human Calibration Loop

Your reviews (Approve, Reject) are crucial. As you catalog findings, they build a local validation set. Once you collect 20 reviews, you can trigger the Calibration engine to optimize the baseline thresholds, reducing false positives dynamically.
